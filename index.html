1. About This Guide

Why I wrote this: To distill what I’ve learned so you can have more favorable experiences.

Important: Your journey is personal. Take what resonates, leave what doesn’t. This is a living guide, not a rulebook.

2. Understanding Magic Mushrooms

What makes them “magic”?

They’re agents of change and transformation. With intention they can help shift your mindset, bring insight, and support lasting states of inner peace.

What are they, literally?

Magic mushrooms contain psilocybin and psilocin, which alter consciousness. They’re being actively studied for mental‑health applications like depression, anxiety, PTSD, and addiction.

  • Expand brain‑network connectivity (neuroplasticity) and help form new connections.
  • May increase gray matter that supports mental functioning.
  • Personally, they can help release tension, blockages, fears, and limiting beliefs; support grief; and deepen meditation/self‑alchemy.

3. Before the Trip

Choosing a Dose

Principle: Match the dose to the inner work at hand. If you haven’t had mushrooms in 6+ months, consider a “full‑trip” dose of ~3 g+.

Cadence Range Typical Effect
Daily 0.25–0.5 g Threshold; light mood shift
Every 2–4 days 0.5–1 g Mild to moderate
Every 4 days 1–2 g Moderate
Once a week 1.5–2.5 g Moderate to strong
Biweekly 2 g+ Strong

Setting Your Intention

Declare your intention 24 hours to 2 weeks in advance. This gives your subconscious time to prepare and distinguishes medicine work from recreation. If mushrooms are part of your regular practice, your “vessel” may already be prepared.

Preparing Your Vessel

Fasting

Goal: Fast for 4–8 hours before ingestion. If hungry, have a light snack (e.g., an apple) about 30 minutes prior.

  • Helps reduce nausea.
  • Can enhance the medicine’s effects by freeing up energy from digestion.

What is Microdosing?

Microdosing: A subperceptual dose <0.1g. It is no greater than a placebo. That is not to say it does not work, but to say it works as well as the power of your mind alone.

4. Day of the Trip

Mental Preparation

Spend 30–60 minutes grounding with practices you’re drawn to: yoga, meditation, prayer, journaling, art, music, walking, or singing. Ingest the mushrooms, then return to grounding practices during the come‑up.

Ingestion Options

Method How Pros Cons
Chew & swallow Chew thoroughly and swallow whole. Simple; intimate; meditative. Taste; possible nausea.
Tea Grind or chop; steep in ~185°F / 85°C water for 15–20 min; add honey/lemon; discard solids. More palatable; often faster onset. Requires time/prep.
Powder with food Mix finely ground mushrooms into applesauce or mashed berries (optional honey/lemon). Very palatable. Needs grinder/mortar or similar.

5. During the Trip

Come‑Up

Impatience, skepticism, anxious anticipation, or excitement are normal. Gently remind yourself you’re safe and everything is unfolding as it should. The come‑up typically lasts 10–45 minutes, depending on ingestion method.

Nausea

Possible but not guaranteed. Fasting and using tea or powder can reduce nausea.

Onset

Lightheadedness or bodily heaviness is common. Sit or lie somewhere comfortable with blankets and pillows.

Main Experience

  • Watch your breath.
  • Follow intuition—thinking, self‑touch, movement, or stillness.
  • If restless or uncomfortable, adjust: change setting, posture, or activity (walk, dance, stretch).
  • Discomfort is a signal to change something—not a test to merely endure.

Eating During

Personal choice. Many wait until after the come‑up. If hungry, choose light, hydrating foods (fruit, soup, salad) and eat until comfortably full.

Best Time of Day

  • Morning: You’re naturally fasted and rested—often ideal.
  • Night: Still and inward but may carry the day’s residue. Avoid starting while sleepy.

6. After the Trip: Integration

Integration means carrying your insights into daily life over the next few days.

7. Final Note

Mushrooms don’t heal you—you heal you. They are a tool that can support your own work. Sometimes you surrender; sometimes you direct. Listen with grace, stay receptive, and let both positive and challenging experiences teach you for future sessions.
